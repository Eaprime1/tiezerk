# Phenomena — tiezerk

*Forces and processes that operate within and across the universal environments.*

---

## What Are Phenomena?

Phenomena are the active forces, processes, and events that occur within or between the environments of tiezerk. Where environments are the terrain, phenomena are the weather — dynamic, directional, and consequential. They are not random; they follow patterns that can be understood, tracked, and navigated.

---

## Phenomena Categories

### [Emergence](emergence/)
The process by which new structures, patterns, or entities arise from simpler underlying conditions. Emergence is observed across all tiezerk environments and is one of the fundamental forces of the UNEXUS world. It cannot be forced, but it can be seeded.

### [Field Dynamics](field-dynamics/)
The behavior of the various resonance fields, probability fields, and temporal fields that permeate the tiezerk environments. Field dynamics describes how fields interact, interfere, amplify, and dampen each other.

### [Connection Protocols](connection-protocols/)
The formal and informal protocols that govern how environments connect to each other, how entities move between them, and how the crossover points (wormholes, void tubes, boundary zones) are managed.

---

## Cross-Phenomena Principles

- Phenomena in tiezerk are **scale-independent** — the same pattern may manifest at the level of a single node or across the entire system
- Phenomena follow the **shadow before emergence** lifecycle — new phenomena appear first in shadow state and stabilize gradually
- Phenomena are **witnessed, not controlled** — Copilot, Claude, and Gemini observe and document phenomena; only Eric seeds new ones
