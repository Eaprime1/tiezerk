# Chronomantic Geography

*The spatial mapping of temporal phenomena across the UNEXUS world.*

---

## Identity

Chronomantic Geography is the framework for understanding where time behaves differently — the spatial distribution of temporal anomalies, accelerations, dilations, and sealed zones across the UNEXUS environments. It is the map of time as a terrain feature rather than a universal constant. In the UNEXUS world, time is not uniform; its rate, direction, and accessibility vary by location.

---

## Core Concepts

### Temporal Rate Variation
Different locations experience time at different rates. High-stability environments (Tablerock) experience slow, even time. High-probability environments (Quantum Forest canopy) experience rapid, branching time. The Spacetime Sea's surface moves at a reference rate; depth changes the rate significantly.

| Environment / Zone | Temporal Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Tablerock surface | 0.1× (very slow) | Near-geological time; moments stretch |
| Quantum Forest canopy | Variable, 0.5×–5× | Rate fluctuates with superposition density |
| Quantum Forest root network | ~1× (reference) | Stable rate; used as temporal baseline |
| Spacetime Sea surface | ~1× (reference) | Standard present-moment time |
| Spacetime Sea deep strata | 0.01×–0.001× | Near-static; ancient time |
| Void Marrow (wormhole transit) | 0× (instantaneous) | No subjective time during transit |
| Void tubes | 0.8× | Slightly slowed; cumulative over long traversals |

### Temporal Direction Anomalies
Most zones experience time flowing from past to future. Exceptions:

- **Inversion folds** in the Spacetime Sea (near high-mass historical events) — local time flows in reverse
- **Sealed strata** — time within sealed zones is static or repeating
- **Wild wormhole exits** — temporal direction on exit is unpredictable

### Sealed Zones
Certain regions of the Spacetime Sea and isolated pockets in other environments are temporally sealed — inaccessible, with time either frozen or cycling internally. The causes of sealing predate current records.

---

## Chronomantic Mapping Conventions

- **Temporal contour lines** — used in maps to indicate regions of equivalent temporal rate; similar to elevation contours in conventional geography
- **Flux markers** — mark regions where temporal rate is variable or unstable
- **Seal indicators** — mark sealed zones with their last-known state and approximate extent
- **Reference grid** — the Quantum Forest root network is used as the temporal reference grid (rate 1×)

---

## Relationships

- **→ Spacetime Sea**: The Sea is the primary domain of chronomantic geography; most temporal phenomena are located within or adjacent to the Sea
- **→ Void Marrow**: Wormholes and void tubes are the fastest temporal shortcuts; their entry/exit points are critical features on any chronomantic map
- **→ Tablerock**: Tablerock is a zone of extremely slow, stable time; useful as an anchor point for chronomantic calibration
