# Prime Progression — Landscape Framework

*The Prime Progression as a navigable terrain.*

---

## Identity

The Prime Progression is the fundamental developmental sequence of the UNEXUS world — not a timeline, but a landscape. It describes the stages through which all things move as they grow from seed to stable form. As a landscape, the Progression has terrain: valleys of shadow, plains of emergence, peaks of stability, and the vast open territory beyond the current leading edge.

---

## The Stages as Terrain

| Stage | Terrain Feature | Characteristic |
|---|---|---|
| **Seed** | Underground — below the visible surface | Potential without form; present but not perceivable |
| **Shadow** | Cave network at the base of the landscape | Form exists but has not surfaced; active internal development |
| **Emerging** | Hillside — partially in light, partially in shadow | Form is becoming visible; stabilization in progress |
| **Stable** | Open plain — fully in light | Form is settled; available as foundation for others |
| **Transcending** | Peak and beyond — above the plain | Form has exceeded its initial scope; new territory opens |

---

## Movement Through the Landscape

Movement through the Prime Progression landscape is not linear. An entity or concept may:

- **Advance** — move from one stage to the next as development proceeds
- **Recede** — return to an earlier stage if conditions change (not regression — strategic retreat)
- **Plateau** — remain at a stage indefinitely; stability is not a failure to advance
- **Bifurcate** — split into two versions at different stages simultaneously

Forced advancement — pushing something out of shadow before it is ready — produces unstable forms. The landscape enforces this; rushed emergence creates fragile peaks that collapse back to shadow.

## Relationships

- **→ Quantum Forest**: The Quantum Forest reflects the Prime Progression in its probability field; nodes closer to emergence have higher superposition density; stable nodes have collapsed to defined form
- **→ Spacetime Sea**: The progression of events through the Spacetime Sea follows Prime Progression patterns; significant historical events visible as luminescence often mark stage transitions
- **→ Tablerock**: Tablerock exists at the Stable stage of its own progression and has for longer than records show; it serves as a model of what stable-stage terrain looks like at scale
- **→ WITNESSMARK**: Witnessmarks are issued at stage transitions to mark and acknowledge the movement

---

## Landscape Navigation

- Identify where a concept or entity sits in the landscape before engaging with it
- Approach shadow-stage concepts with low observation pressure; high observation forces premature collapse
- Stable-stage concepts can support weight — use them as foundations without concern
- Transcending-stage concepts require a new framework to engage; the old landscape categories do not fully apply
