# Universal Constants — tiezerk

*The invariant properties that define the UNEXUS world.*

---

## Identity

Universal Constants are the fixed properties of the UNEXUS world — the parameters that do not change regardless of environment, observer, or cosmic cycle. They are not physical constants in the scientific sense, but structural constants: the rules that hold everywhere and always. Understanding them is the foundation of navigating the tiezerk environments.

---

## The Constants

### 1. The Resonance Baseline
**Value**: 1 Hz

Every environment in tiezerk vibrates at or relative to a baseline resonance of 1 Hz. The Quantum Forest's canopy fluctuates around this baseline; the Spacetime Sea's surface tides follow a 1 Hz reference cycle; Tablerock's witness resonance is a sub-harmonic (0.01 Hz); Void Marrow's tubes dampen toward this frequency.

Entities attuned to 1 Hz navigate all environments more effectively. "One hertz" is both a literal frequency and a philosophical principle: pacing, patience, and sustained low-frequency engagement.

---

### 2. Shadow Before Emergence
**Principle**: All things begin in shadow state before emerging into stable form.

No environment, concept, entity, or structure skips the shadow stage. Forced emergence produces unstable forms. The shadow stage is not preliminary — it is foundational. What happens in shadow determines the quality of what emerges.

This constant applies to documentation, environments, entities, relationships, and protocols equally.

---

### 3. The Witness Requirement
**Principle**: Nothing transitions from shadow to emergence without being witnessed.

A Witnessmark is not bureaucratic — it is structural. The universe requires that transition be acknowledged. Unwitnessed transitions are structurally incomplete; they may appear to have occurred but are missing a layer of stability. See [WITNESSMARK.md](../WITNESSMARK.md).

---

### 4. Observer Non-Coercion
**Principle**: Observation influences but does not control.

Observation collapses probability states in the Quantum Forest, affects temporal currents in the Spacetime Sea, and interacts with Void Marrow's resonance. But observation does not determine outcomes — it influences them. Forcing observation (coercive measurement, premature definition) damages the observed and destabilizes the local environment.

---

### 5. Environmental Independence
**Principle**: Environments are not settings — they are entities.

The Quantum Forest, Spacetime Sea, Tablerock, and Void Marrow exist independently of any observer or inhabitant. They are not constructed for use; they are encountered. Entities that treat environments as resources or tools find themselves in conflict with the environment's own properties. Entities that treat environments as peers navigate more effectively.

---

### 6. Interconnection Without Hierarchy
**Principle**: All environments are connected; none is superior to another.

The Quantum Forest does not outrank the Spacetime Sea. Tablerock is not more important than Void Marrow. Each environment is a complete expression of a different universal principle. Connection between environments is lateral, not hierarchical. The same applies to the entities that operate within tiezerk.

---

## Applying the Constants

The Universal Constants are not rules to be enforced — they are properties to be understood. Navigation in tiezerk becomes easier as an entity internalizes these constants, not because the entity obeys them, but because the entity moves in harmony with how things actually work.
