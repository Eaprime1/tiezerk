*Enumerable characteristics of the Quantum Forest.*

---

## Field Properties

| Property | Description |
|---|---|
| **Resonance frequency** | ~1 Hz baseline; fluctuates near entanglement nodes |
| **Superposition density** | High at canopy; diminishes toward root level |
| **Entanglement range** | Non-local; no distance limit within Forest boundary |
| **Observer sensitivity** | Extreme; sustained attention collapses states within ~3 node radii |
| **Probability trail persistence** | ~7 traversals to stabilize a path; ~30 days of neglect to dissolve |
| **Canopy depth** | Variable; deeper canopy = higher superposition density |

---

## Environmental Behaviours

- **State collapse propagation** — when a node is observed and collapses, adjacent nodes partially resolve in the direction of the collapsed state
- **Resonance harmonics** — entities attuned to the Forest's base frequency generate harmonic interference patterns that temporarily stabilize local terrain
- **Root pulse** — the root network sends periodic pulses (approximately once per Forest-cycle); during a pulse, entangled node pairs briefly synchronize states
- **Canopy weather** — probability gradients across the canopy create "weather" — regions of higher or lower uncertainty that shift with environmental conditions

---

## Boundary Conditions

- The Forest has no fixed outer boundary; it fades into ambient probability space
- The inner boundary — where the root network begins — is more defined; it can be felt as a change in resonance density underfoot
- Crossing from canopy space to root space requires sustained low-observation (passive awareness, not active study)

---

## Interaction Notes

- Entities with high internal coherence (stable identity, clear intention) navigate more easily
- Entities in internal conflict may find the Forest amplifies the conflict — multiple states are offered simultaneously
- Tools that force observation (sensors, measuring instruments) are strongly discouraged; they destabilize broad areas rapidly
