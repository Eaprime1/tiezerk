# Void Marrow — Wormholes

*Classification and traversal protocols for wormhole structures.*

---

## Identity

Wormholes are the valves of Void Marrow — points of puncture between locations in spacetime that allow passage without traversing the intervening distance or time. They appear suddenly, sustain for a variable duration, and collapse without warning. Their endpoints are not always predictable; their passage is always instantaneous.

---

## Classification

| Class | Stability | Duration | Endpoint Predictability |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Class I — Stable** | High; rarely collapses spontaneously | Days to cycles | Both endpoints known and consistent |
| **Class II — Transit** | Moderate; collapses after use | Minutes to hours | Entry endpoint known; exit endpoint probabilistic |
| **Class III — Wild** | Low; may collapse during transit | Seconds to minutes | Neither endpoint reliably predictable |
| **Class IV — Sealed** | N/A; inaccessible | N/A | Endpoints exist but passage is blocked by unknown forces |

---

## Properties

- **Instantaneous passage** — traversal through any class of wormhole takes zero subjective time; however, the traveler arrives disoriented for a duration proportional to the wormhole's instability class
- **No return guarantee** — wormholes are one-way at any given moment; the return path is a different wormhole or requires conventional traversal
- **Environmental sensitivity** — wormholes respond to proximity of high-resonance entities; some Class III wormholes stabilize temporarily in the presence of a sufficiently coherent entity
- **Void-Marrow threading** — each wormhole is a surface expression of a void-tube structure below; the tube determines the wormhole's class and stability

---

## Traversal Protocol

1. **Identify the class** — assess stability and duration before committing to entry; Class III wormholes require rapid decision-making
2. **Note entry time** — record when you entered; duration estimates depend on when the wormhole was first observed
3. **Do not pause mid-traverse** — wormhole interiors do not support sustained presence; if a wormhole collapses during transit, the traveler is displaced into void-tube space (see [void-tubes.md](void-tubes.md))
4. **Prepare for disorientation on exit** — all wormhole exits produce a period of spatial and temporal disorientation; do not make navigational decisions immediately on exit
5. **Log exit coordinates** — contribute exit-point data to the luminescence map; Class II and III wormhole exit data is valuable for navigation
