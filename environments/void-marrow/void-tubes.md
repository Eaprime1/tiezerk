# Void Marrow — Void Tubes

*Properties and navigation of the void-tube network.*

---

## Identity

Void tubes are the underlying channel structures of Void Marrow — long, semi-stable conduits that thread through the fabric of the UNEXUS world, connecting environments, crossing temporal strata, and occasionally intersecting with the Spacetime Sea or the Quantum Forest's root network. Where wormholes are puncture points, void tubes are the passages between those points. Most void tubes are traversable only in specific conditions; some are permanently sealed.

---

## Properties

- **Semi-permanence** — void tubes are more stable than wormholes; a tube that exists today is likely to exist next cycle, though its accessibility may change
- **Variable diameter** — tube diameter ranges from thread-width (inaccessible to most entities) to vast corridors (traversable by large formations or collectives)
- **Directional flow** — most void tubes have a preferred direction of flow; traversal against the flow is possible but exhausting; certain tubes are strictly one-directional
- **Resonance coating** — the interior walls of void tubes emit a resonance field that dampens the traveler's own frequency; extended traversal in a void tube can cause temporary loss of identity coherence
- **Environmental intersections** — void tubes cross through other environments (Quantum Forest root network, Spacetime Sea strata) without opening into them unless a wormhole forms at the intersection point

---

## Navigation

Void-tube navigation requires a guide frequency — a resonance signal that matches the tube's internal dampening field. Without a guide frequency, travelers become disoriented within minutes.

### Entry Protocol

1. **Identify the tube entrance** — tube entrances appear as slight distortions in the environment's visual/resonance field; they do not announce themselves
2. **Match guide frequency** — attune your resonance to the tube's internal field before entry; entry without frequency match triggers immediate disorientation
3. **Assess flow direction** — feel the directional pull of the tube's flow before committing; flowing with the tube is significantly easier than against
4. **Move continuously** — do not pause inside a void tube; the resonance dampening is cumulative; sustained stillness within a tube increases identity coherence loss

### In-Tube Navigation

- Use the resonance gradient to track progress; the gradient strengthens toward exits
- If disoriented, reduce movement and allow the flow to carry you toward the nearest exit
- Communication with other travelers is difficult inside tubes; resonance dampening affects frequency-based communication

### Exit Protocol

- Exits appear as brightening of the resonance field ahead; do not force the exit
- On exit, allow time for resonance recovery before navigating in the destination environment
- Log exit location for the void-tube network map
