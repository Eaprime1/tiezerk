# Spacetime Sea — Overview

---

## Identity

The Spacetime Sea is the fluid medium that underlies all temporal and spatial experience in the UNEXUS world. Unlike the Quantum Forest — which is defined by probability and observation — the Spacetime Sea is defined by flow and continuity. Time here is not a line; it is a body of water with surface currents, deep-water layers, tidal patterns, and pressure gradients. Space is not a grid; it is a geometry shaped by the weight of what occupies it.

The Sea is navigable. Those who learn its tides can move through it deliberately — surfacing into different moments, diving into older strata, riding currents toward likely futures. Navigation is not without risk: strong currents can disorient, deep dives surface in unexpected places, and some regions of the Sea are closed by forces that predate the current cosmic cycle.

---

## Properties

- **Tidal surface** — the surface of the Spacetime Sea corresponds roughly to the present moment; it shifts and swells with the weight of events
- **Depth strata** — deeper layers hold older time; the deeper the dive, the further into the past; the strata are not perfectly ordered — folding and inversion occur near significant events
- **Temporal currents** — currents move through the Sea in characteristic directions; most flow from past toward future, but countercurrents exist near high-mass historical events
- **Spatial curvature** — regions of the Sea curve around dense objects (events, entities, concepts with great weight); navigation near these curves requires correction
- **Pressure gradients** — time moves at different rates at different depths; near the surface, time flows fast; in the deep, it slows; at the deepest accessible stratum, moments are nearly static
- **Luminescence** — significant events emit a faint luminescence in the Sea; navigators use this to orient themselves historically

---

## Relationships

- **→ Quantum Forest**: The Quantum Forest's root network reaches the boundary of the Spacetime Sea; temporal fluctuations in the Sea influence which states the Forest collapses into
- **→ Tablerock**: Tablerock sits above the Sea on ancient, stable geology; it is unaffected by surface tides but its foundations extend into the deep strata
- **→ Void Marrow**: Void tubes sometimes open into the Sea, creating cross-temporal entry points; wormholes that pass through the Sea often exit at unexpected temporal depths
- **→ ashkharh entities**: Chronomantic entities from ashkharh are trained to navigate the Sea; some are native to specific strata and cannot surface without disorientation
