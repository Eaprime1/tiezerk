## Tides

Tides in the Spacetime Sea may be spatial, temporal, emotional, or Ka-driven.

A tide may:

- reveal a hidden route
- obscure a visible route
- return unresolved wreckage
- intensify Pathos, Phrike, or Achos
- expose a false calm
- carry a signal through the dark medium
- bring a voyager near a threshold of ascent or descent

## Doldrums and Aldus

A doldrum is not merely stillness.

Stillness can be sacred when it remains alive. A vessel may hold position to heal, listen, grieve, or wait for the tide.

A doldrum becomes Aldus when:

1. rest no longer answers motion;
2. comfort becomes refusal;
3. pressure no longer transforms;
4. grief stops circulating;
5. the vessel mistakes entombment for peace.

---

## Sargasso Zones

Sargasso zones are areas of accumulation and entanglement.

They may hold:

- wreckage
- memory fragments
- abandoned signals
- unresolved grief
- net-threads
- vessel remnants
- truths not yet safe to surface

Sargasso is not automatically hostile. It may be a sorting field, composting field, archive, or trap depending on whether motion remains possible.

Seed distinction:

```text
Sargasso gathers.
Aldus arrests.
Altarum shelters.
Altum contains.
```

Ka-pressure is a hidden navigational force generated by essence, vigor, and will.

A voyager may perceive Ka-pressure as:

- pull
- ache
- shiver
- compression
- resonance
- refusal
- invitation
- change in emotional weather

Ka-pressure may originate from a being, vessel, memory, collision, invocation, or threshold.

## Ascent and Descent

In Altum, ascent and descent are not always vertical.

**Descent** may mean:

- entering protected darkness
- relinquishing surface identity
- allowing pressure to equalize grief
- becoming less visible but more deeply held

**Ascent** may mean:

- accepting relation
- becoming visible enough to be needed
- bringing depth-wisdom into motion
- allowing comfort to become service

The strongest ascent does not abandon the deep. It carries the deep forward.
