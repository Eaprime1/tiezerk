# tiezerk → ashkharh

*How tiezerk environments connect to individual entities and characters.*

## The Relationship

tiezerk defines the universal environments — the terrain. ashkharh defines the individual entities — the inhabitants and travelers. These two repositories are complementary and interdependent: environments without entities are unexplored; entities without environments are ungrounded.

The connection is not ownership. tiezerk does not own the characters that inhabit its environments. ashkharh does not own the environments its characters traverse. The relationship is one of mutual definition: environments and entities shape each other through interaction.

---

## How Characters Enter tiezerk Environments

When an ashkharh entity is described as being within a tiezerk environment (e.g., "Navigo Nexusuxen is in the Quantum Forest"), the physics of that environment apply. The character's properties interact with the environment's properties according to the Universal Constants and the specific dynamics of that environment.

This is not mechanical — it is relational. The Quantum Forest responds differently to different entities. Tablerock is equally stable regardless of who sits on it. Void Marrow opens only when it chooses to. The environment's own character is always primary.
